ROYAL STARTERS

Golden Nkwobi Tartare Signature Dish
Succulent cow foot delicately diced, mixed with palm oil cream, and served in crispy plantain cups with a side of fermented ugba relish.

The Art of Nkwobi

Our cow feet are sourced from Orie Awka market where butchers use traditional cleavers. The palm oil comes from Chief Nwankwo's grove in Abagana, cold-pressed within 24 hours of harvest. The plantain cups are made by Mrs. Obi's cooperative using unripe plantains from Nnewi farms.

"We select only the freshest cow legs - you can hear the cartilage sing when we cut it!"
- Mazi Emeka, Master Butcher

Ofe Owerri Royale Chef's Legacy
A luxurious deconstruction of the classic: slow-cooked assorted meats (goat, tripe, and bush meat) in an ultra-smooth cocoyam base, topped with crispy uziza flower tempura. Served with three-texture pounded yam (foam, crisp, and traditional).

Sacred Soup of the Ancestors

Our cocoyam comes from sacred plots in Owerri Nkworji, cultivated with the same methods used by King Eze Njemanze's cooks. The uziza flowers are hand-picked at dawn by Mgboto women's collective, while the bush meat is ethically sourced from Okigwe forest reserves.

"This recipe came to me in a dream when I was 12 - the spirits of my grandmother taught me the secret of the perfect draw!"
- Madam Chioma, Soup Oracle
